This is my variation of this Neil Young song. It's played sorta similar
  to what Trey plays but it is in no way an exact interpretation. In all
  places where more than one note is being struck at a time I am using the
  open fingers not holding the pick. It gives it a nice sound. The timing
  is hard to get but if you have a copy of the song just make sure the
  chords line up and it will sound fine. Basically on the D chords I am just
  picking the chord in about as many ways I can think of, there is no right
  way to do it. I hope that this is a helpful guide to you. I play what is
  written as the intro, and for the verses I strum the chords. The chorus
  I do not strum the chords I play the tab, but that is completely personal
  preference. For the solo I play the intro again, and for the last verse
  I strum the chords. This is a great song to play with no band accompanyment.


Mike (niteshade@iquest.net)
