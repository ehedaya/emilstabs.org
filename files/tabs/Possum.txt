I finally figured out exactly what Trey plays.  If this isn't it, then I don't
know what it is.  I listened to the 11/3/98 radio interview, and he was kind of
noodling the intro while he was talking, right before they played an acoustic
version of it live on the radio.  This was what he played, and it was really easy
to hear.  Enjoy.

== Opening From Live Phish #9 ==


This is a fairly complicated opening that Trey plays, so I'll just list the
basics without getting too much into the fills and timings of what he plays.
When I first heard this, I went nuts, and was drying to know how he plays it.
I think I just figured it out!

He hits some pretty interesting parts though, makes me wonder why he doesn't
jam out like this anymore :(

This part is played with upstrokes, mute all the strings other than the two
you are playing.  If you listen to the recording, you can hear exactly how he
moves between these fingerings, it's pretty linear:


                       [these just slide down]
E--x---x---x---x---x---x---x---x---x------
B--x---x---x---x---x---x---x---x---x------
G--13--11--9---7---6---4---3---2---1------
D--x---x---x---x---x---x---x---x---x------
A--14--12--11--9---7---5---4---3---2------
E--x---x---x---x---x---x---x---x---x------


Then he does some noodling around these little riffs/licks:

               B         H-O
E-15-15-15-15-15--------------------------------------
B-14-14-14-14-14^15--14--12---------------------------
G--------------------14--12-13------------------------
D------------------------------14-12-(trails off)-----
A-----------------------------------------------------
E-----------------------------------------------------

The rest is pretty standard trey-style soloing, bending and stuff.
You didn't want me to do ALL the work, did you?  Play around with it, it's
not hard to figure out and very rewarding :)
